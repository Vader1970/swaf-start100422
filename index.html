The story of Kenner and Rare Action Figures

google trademe

Kenner realised that they wouldnt be able to have any action figures available to the public before Christmas 1977. Star Wars - A New Hope had already been released and was a massive hit all over the world. Young children were waiting with great anticipation to get their hands on any action figurines.

As an alternative, Kenner offered for sale an Early Bird Mail-Away Certificate where the purchaser could send the certificate in and was promised that they will be the very first people to receive Star Wars action figures. Therefore the very first Star Wars action figures were released in 1978 as part of the mail-away Star Wars Early Bird Kit, which contained Star Wars figures of Luke Skywalker, Princess Leia, Chewbacca and R2-D2.

The last figures in the series were released during the Power of the Force series in 1985, before Kenner released figures based on the Droids and Ewoks cartoon TV shows. After that there was a ten year break until Hasbro started to produce Star Wars figures again in 1995.

Over the years Star Wars toys have been very popular with collectors from all over the globe. Collectors will now pay astounding amount of money to have an item within their collection. The rarer the item; the higher it will cost you to obtain it. There are even rarer variants of the same figure that have sold for enormous amount of money. Just an example is the J-Slot Firing Boba Fett graded at AFA 50, sold for $204,435 on Hakes.com auction March 16th, 2022.

This webpage will list some of the rarer vintage Star Wars toys that collectors would consider to be Holy Grails.
