Rocket Firing Boba Fett

The famous Rocket Firing Boba Fett is the Holy Grail to majority of Star Wars Collectors.

In 1978, Kenner started developing a Boba Fett that would fire a projector missile.

People who collected the first Carded Star Wars figures released, were given the option to mail away 3 purchases of proof and they would be the very first customers to receive the new action figure (Boba Fett with a Rocket Firing Mechanism) from the new unseen movie The Empire Strikes Back.

Kenner made 100 prototypes of the figure and they were shipped off to China for testing. These tests were quite extreme where the figures were tested at extreme high and very low temperatures. Only 27 survived and collectors have only been able to account for 24 of them.

However, in the final stages of the development of the figure; a child had choked and died from having a BattleStar Galactica vehicle projectile missile lodge in his throat. Therefore Kenner decided not to have the firing mechanism and all the customers that had mailed in for the figure receive the figure with the rocket glued in place. The firing mechanism was completed taken away.

There are two major variants which exist and they are the L-Slot and J-Slot rocket firing mechanism. The J-Slot Boba Fett being the rarer of the two. A J-Slot Boba Fett graded at 50% quality just sold on www.Hakes.com website for an astounding $204,435.

Each prototype figure was loose; however a carded one did show up at the Toy fair Expo. This was the only fully completed Rocket Firing Boba Fett ever made.
